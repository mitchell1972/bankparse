Bank Statement Conversion for Audits

Audits need complete, accurate bank statement data — not manual retyping. Here's how AI conversion speeds up audit fieldwork.

Audit fieldwork means processing large volumes of client bank statements. Whether it's a statutory audit, an internal review, or a grant audit, you need transaction data in an analysable format — and you need it fast. Manual data entry introduces errors and eats into billable audit hours.

Why Bank Statement Conversion Matters for Audits

  • Sampling: Auditors need complete transaction lists to select representative samples for testing
  • Reconciliation: Bank statement data must be reconciled against the client's general ledger — this is impossible with PDFs alone
  • Analytical procedures: Ratio analysis, trend analysis, and flux analysis all require structured data
  • Documentation: ISA 500 requires auditors to document the evidence obtained — a clean spreadsheet is auditable; a stack of PDFs is not

Time saving for audit teams: A medium-sized audit typically involves 15-25 client bank accounts with 12 months of statements each — that's 180-300 PDFs. Manual extraction at 10 minutes each = 30-50 hours. AI conversion reduces this to under 1 hour of processing time.

The Audit Workflow with AI Conversion

  1. Request statements: Ask the client to download 12 months of bank statement PDFs for all relevant accounts
  2. Batch convert: Upload all PDFs to BankScan AI and receive formatted Excel files
  3. Import into audit software: Load the structured data into CaseWare, CCH, Mercia, or Excel-based work papers
  4. Perform procedures: With clean data, sampling, reconciliation, and analysis are straightforward
