The Manual Data Entry Problem

The average UK bookkeeper spends 10–15 hours per week on repetitive data entry: downloading bank statements, copying figures into Excel, categorising transactions, and reconciling accounts. At £25–35 per hour, that's over £1,000 a month in low-value work per staff member.

Why 2026 Is the Tipping Point for UK Firms

Three forces are converging to make accounting automation essential this year:

1. The talent shortage is getting worse

UK accounting firms are struggling to recruit. According to ICAEW, the number of students starting accountancy qualifications has dropped for three consecutive years. Automation isn't optional — it's how firms maintain capacity without hiring.

2. Making Tax Digital is expanding

HMRC's MTD requirements now cover more businesses, demanding digital record-keeping and quarterly submissions. Firms that automate bank statement processing can onboard MTD clients faster and at lower cost.

3. Client expectations have shifted

Business owners now expect real-time financial visibility, not monthly reports delivered two weeks late. AI-powered workflows deliver the speed clients demand.
